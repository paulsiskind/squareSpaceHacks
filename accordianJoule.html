1.0 Introduction


Thank you for choosing the Joule Heat Energy Meter Kit by Amatis Controls. This Installation Manual and User Guide will guide you through the simple process of installing each component of your new Joule Kit and provide instructions to register the device on the web-based Energy Dashboard. Within an hour or so your system will be reporting valuable energy data to the Amatis Energy Dashboard, which you can access online. All components must be installed correctly to ensure the overall accuracy of this kit.




1.1 Before You Start

Your Joule Heat Energy Meter Kit has 4 major components: the JOULE heat meter, a flow sensor, two temperature sensors, and one Amatis Border Router (AMBR). Before beginning your installation, make sure the installation site has:

  • High speed Internet connection (configured for DHCP)
  • Ethernet and electric outlets for AMBR (within 200ft of meter)
  • Electrical outlet (within 6ft of JOULE meter & sensors) (or 9-30V)

2.0 Installing AMBR

AMBR connects your JOULE system to the Internet. Follow the steps below:

  1. Connect one end of the blue Cat5/Ethernet wire to the back of AMBR.
  2. Connect the other end of the blue Cat5/Ethernet wire to an available Ethernet port. NOTE: Some cable & DSL modems have only one Ethernet port. In order to connect AMBR you may need to add another Ethernet port. Ethernet Switches are available to add more Ethernet ports to the same Internet connection. One option is illustrated below:
  3. Plug the barrel end of the white 5V power supply into the back of AMBR and the plug into the wall socket.
  4. Wait for 1-2 minutes while AMBR boots up and connects to the Internet.
  5. On the front of AMBR a blue blinking light confirms AMBR has booted up correctly and is connected to the Internet. You can move on to Section 3.0 INSTALLING TEMPERATURE SENSORS.
  6. If AMBR does not show a blinking blue light after 5 minutes, you may be experiencing an Internet connectivity problem. Follow the troubleshooting steps in the next section.



2.1 Troubleshooting AMBR Connectivity

  1. Restart your modem and AMBR.
    1. Power both devices off
    2. Power modem on
    3. After modem reboots and shows Internet activity
    4. Plug AMBR back in
    5. Verify AMBR shows blue blinking light
  2. Confirm your modem is configured in DHCP mode.
    1. Follow modem owner’s manual directions
    2. Contact your Internet service provider
  3. Identify which AMBR indicator lights are active. NOTE: When AMBR is connected to power, all lights turn on, then turn off, then turn back on one-by-one. Indicators can be diagnosed in the table below:

3.0 Installing Temperature Sensors

Successful temperature sensor installation involves pipe work and soldering. An experienced plumber may be required. Follow the steps below:

  1. Identify the “supply” and “return” pipes for you water heating system. NOTE: A diagram for a typical installation is below:
  2. Make sure your system has been drained before starting temperature sensor installation.




    3. Note: Joule Heat Meters are designed to work with hydronic systems with any type of heat source. Joule does not require solar collectors.




  3. Cut “supply” pipe in location where brass wye and temperature sensors will be located. (See 4.0 INSTALLING FLOW SENSOR)
  4. Insert brass wye for supply side (with arrows on the wye corresponding to the flow of fluid) and solder both sides of the assembly to the pipe.
  5. Cut “return” pipe in location where brass wye and temperature sensors will be located. NOTE: Amatis recommends that the return side brass wye and temperature sensors are close to the storage tank, leaving room for the flow sensor assembly also installed on the return side. See Section 4.0.

  6. Insert brass wye for return side (with arrows on the wye corresponding to the flow of fluid) and solder both sides of the assembly to the pipe.
  7. Apply Teflon thread tape or high temperature rated sealant to thread on both thermo-well inserts. NOTE: Appropriate sealants include: Permatex high temp thread sealant (NAPA); Loctite 5770 high temp sealant; and Loctite 567 PST thread sealant.
  8. Insert and tighten thermo-wells into both brass wye fittings.
  9. Coat the metal tips of both temperature sensors with a generous amount of thermal paste compound and insert the coated temperature sensors into each of the thermo-wells, making sure they reach the bottom of the thermo-well.
  10. Temperature sensors will be connected, via the RJ11 plugs, to the JOULE meter. See Section 5.0 JOULE HEAT METER INSTALLATION.



4.0 Installing Flow Sensor

The flow sensor assembly is supplied in multiple parts. Two brass flow sensor fittings are provided to fit flow sensor to pipes and two large clips hold the sensor to these fittings.



NOTE: Amatis provides different of flow meter sizes – please confirm you have the correct sized flow sensor for your system. IMPORTANT: Flow sensors must be at least 5x (pipe diameter) in length from a bend, pump, or fitting for accuracy. (e.g. 1 inch diameter pipe = 5 inches pipe length on each side of sensor)

Successful flow sensor installation involves pipe work. An experienced plumber may be required. Follow the steps below:

  1. Cut “supply” pipe in location where flow sensor assembly will be located. NOTE: Accurate readings require at least 5 x pipe diameter length from bends, pumps or fittings on both sides
  2. Solder appropriately sized threaded unions to existing pipe on supply side
  3. Attach the low sensor fittings to the threaded unions
  4. Insert flow sensor assembly (with arrows on the sensor corresponding to the flow of fluid)
  5. Using the two large clips to connect the flow sensor to the fittings
  6. Clean all pipes of soldering remnants and all other debris. NOTE: Be extremely careful not to expose flow sensor to any cleaning agents.
  7. Connect the 4-pin sensor wire to the flow sensor after soldering, and all cleaning is complete.

    8. NOTE: the 4-pin connector fits snugly into the flow sensor. Once attached the plastic clips will engage to hold the 4- pin connecter in place.

  8. Ground the flow meter by attaching the grounding strap to the pipework next to the flow sensor. Tighten nut on ground strap to ground cable hookterminal. The ground cable will attach to the RJ11 input on the Joule meter.
  9. The RJ11 plug on the opposite end of the flow sensor wire will connect to the JOULE heat meter. See next section.



5.0 Installing Joule Heat Meter

Once all sensors are installed properly, successful JOULE installation is plug-and-play. Simply follow the steps below:

  1. Plug flow sensor wire into the RJ11 terminal labeled “Flow Meter.”
  2. Plug supply temperature sensor wire into RJ11 terminal labeled “Supply Temp.”
  3. Plug return temperature sensor wire into RJ11 terminal labeled “Return Temp.”
  4. Plug grounding wire into RJ11 terminal labeled “Ground.”
  5. Plug JOULE power supply into a nearby outlet.
  6. Indicator lights on JOULE will begin to light up.
  7. Confirm JOULE is reading flow by manually controlling the pump and looking for the Yellow LED indicator. Indicator will be on with flow greater than 1.5 L/min

5.2 Troubleshooting Joule Connectivity

Power supply ERROR:

  • Verify wall outlet is live
  • Check 12v power supplt connections

Flow Sensor ERROR:

  • At least 1.5L/m flow must be running
  • Check flow sensor connections

Temperature sensor Error or Fault:

  • Check tempature sensor connections
  • Check thermo-well fittings
  • Check wye installation

Internet Connections Error:

  • Restart JOULE(unplug and plug-in)
  • Check wireless ranfge limits or obstacles
  • To test for range limits or obstacles, remove all 4 RJ11 inputs and relocate the Joule meter within 50ft of AMBR and power up. Note whether the blue light turns on. Locate areas further away from AMBR to continue checking wireless signal. If Joule location is out of range or experiencing interference your installation may require an Amatis Lilly Pad to bounce the signal back to AMBR.
